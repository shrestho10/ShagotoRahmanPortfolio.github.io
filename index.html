About Me

Hi I am Shagoto Rahman, PhD Student at UC Irvine, specializing in Large Language Models and Responsible AI. My research spans jailbreak defense in LLMs, multi-intent detection using fine-tuned transformers, and building chatbot systems that identify and summarize the most relevant responses. I’ve worked extensively on parameter-efficient fine-tuning (LoRA, QLoRA), and am currently exploring hallucination-free generation and scalable, responsible AI systems. Through my work, I aim to improve the reliability, safety, and practical utility of generative models across real-world tasks.
